Success Metrics

Signals, not targets

Whether the task layer is working — the signals we'll watch once Conductor is live. Targets are out of scope for this outline; they get set at the alignment meeting with the PM + TA leads, informed by the current ops baseline.

Metric What it tells us Why it matters
Suggestion acceptance rate % of AI-proposed suggestions that operators approve (vs dismiss or ignore until expiry) Trust signal. Low = noise; high = the suggestion model is calibrated to real work.
Auto-approved task accuracy For tasks that bypass human review, % completed without reopen / correction / complaint Tells us where the auto-tier confidence threshold is right vs too aggressive.
False positive rate % of all AI-generated suggestions that turn out not to be tasks at all (operators dismiss as noise) The fatigue signal. Above the noise floor and operators stop reading suggestions.
Time to first action Time from task creation to first operator touch (or AI auto-execution) Speed signal. Especially important on World-driven (flight delays, weather) where minutes matter.
Completion / closure rate % of created tasks closed within the expected window for their origin bucket Throughput signal. Stuck-task accumulation is the early indicator of process drift.
Reduction in missed operational moments Count of post-hoc-detected misses (a moment we should have caught but didn't) The hardest metric, partly self-reported, partly client-reported. The whole point of the proactive schedule-driven side is to drive this down.

Signals, not targets

Each signal needs a baseline (where are we today?) and a target (where do we want it after N weeks of Conductor live?). Both belong in the PRD, not this outline.
